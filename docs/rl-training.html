Dedicated RL Training Page

Training the RL Dynamic-Obstacle Avoidance Policy from Scratch

This page documents the learning component of NavRL Bench: training a PPO-based local navigation policy for dynamic-obstacle avoidance in IsaacLab / Isaac Sim, exporting the trained policy, and integrating it into the ROS2 / Nav2 local control pipeline.

Back to Project Home Training Pipeline

Why this page exists

RL training is the main engineering contribution

The classical controllers are available Nav2 baselines. The RL controller is different because it is trained from scratch as part of this project.

This page separates the RL training work from the benchmark page so that the project clearly shows environment design, policy training, reward shaping, policy export, and deployment planning.

Training Environment

IsaacLab / Isaac Sim setup

Robot

ROSMASTER M3 is used as the robot platform for training and benchmark consistency.

Environment

The training environment is designed around dynamic obstacle avoidance and goal-directed movement.

Training Goal

The policy should learn to output safe and useful velocity commands for avoiding dynamic obstacles while navigating toward the goal.

Policy Interface

Observation and action design

Planned Observation Inputs

  • Goal direction relative to the robot
  • Goal distance
  • Robot linear velocity
  • Robot angular velocity
  • Laser scan or compact local obstacle representation
  • Previous action for smoother behavior
  • Optional local path information from Nav2

Planned Action Output

  • Linear velocity command
  • Angular velocity command
  • Action limits matched to ROSMASTER M3 safety limits
  • Output format compatible with /cmd_vel

Reward Design

Training signal for navigation behavior

Positive Rewards

  • Progress toward goal
  • Reaching the goal
  • Maintaining useful forward motion
  • Following safe local navigation behavior

Penalties

  • Collision with obstacles
  • Getting too close to obstacles
  • Unnecessary stopping
  • Moving away from the goal
  • Jerky or unstable velocity commands

Reward terms will be updated based on training experiments. The final reward design will be documented with configuration files and ablation notes.

Training Strategy

Curriculum toward fast dynamic-obstacle avoidance

The final target is fast dynamic-obstacle avoidance. Simple goal reaching and static obstacles are used as curriculum stages before introducing moving obstacles.

01

Simple Goal Reaching

The policy first learns basic movement toward a goal without complex obstacles.

02

Static Obstacle Avoidance

The environment introduces fixed obstacles so the policy learns safe local detours.

03

Dynamic Obstacles

Moving obstacles are added to evaluate adaptive behavior such as slowing, passing, or avoiding.

04

Domain Randomization

Training can randomize obstacle placement, start-goal pairs, friction, and sensor noise.

05

Policy Validation

The trained policy is tested in simulation before it is compared with Nav2 classical controllers.

06

Export and Integration

The final policy is exported and loaded by the RL local controller for benchmark evaluation.

Training Deliverables

Artifacts from the RL training pipeline

The RL training pipeline generates the policy checkpoints, exported policy files, training curves, validation logs, and simulation videos required to verify the learning process.

These artifacts help demonstrate how the policy was trained, how its behavior evolved during training, and how it is prepared for integration into the Nav2 local controller pipeline.

Connection to Benchmark

How the trained policy is evaluated

After training, the policy is integrated into the ROS2 / Nav2 local-control pipeline and evaluated against classical Nav2 controllers under the same ROSMASTER M3 benchmark scenarios.

This keeps the final comparison fair: classical controllers use their Nav2 implementations, while the RL controller uses the trained policy produced by this training pipeline.
